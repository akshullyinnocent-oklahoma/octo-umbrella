ORDER AFFIRMING DENIAL OF APPLICATION FOR POST-CONVICTION RELIEF

The Petitioner has appealed, pro se, to this Court from an order of the District Court of Cleveland County denying his application for post-conviction relief in Case No. CF-2019-1273. Petitioner was tried by a jury and found guilty of two counts of lewd or indecent acts with a child under sixteen. 

The jury assessed punishment at twenty-five years imprisonment in each count. The Honorable Thad Balkman, District Judge, pronounced judgment and ordered the sentences served consecutively, suspending all but eighteen years of the sentence in Count 1 and the entire twenty-five year sentence in Count 2. 

This Court affirmed Petitioner’s judgment and sentence on direct appeal. Norton v. State, No. F-2022-746 (Okl.Cr. July 3, 2024)(not for publication) 

Petitioner filed an application for post-conviction relief in the district court, raising ineffective assistance of appellate counsel, factual innocence, and cumulative error. Judge Balkman denied post-conviction relief in an order filed on February 13, 2026. Petitioner now appeals.

We review the district court’s determination for an abuse of discretion. State ex rel. Smith v. Neuwirth, 2014 OK CR 16, ¥ 12, 337 P.3d 763, 766. An abuse of discretion is any unreasonable or arbitrary action taken without proper consideration of the facts and law pertaining to the matter at issue or a clearly erroneous conclusion and judgment, one that is clearly against the logic and effect of the facts presented. Neloms v. State, 2012 OK CR 7, § 35, 274 P.3d 161, 170. “In the interests of efficiency and finality, our judicial system employs various doctrines to ensure that issues are not endlessly re-litigated.” Smith v. State, 2013 OK CR 14, J 14, 306 P.3d 557, 564. 

All issues that were previously raised and ruled upon in direct appeal proceedings or previous post-conviction proceedings are barred as res judicata, and all issues that could have been raised in those previous proceedings but were not, are waived, and may not be the basis of a subsequent post-conviction application. King v. State, 2001 OK CR 22, § 4, 29 P.3d 1089, 1090. 

Post-conviction review is not an opportunity for a second chance to argue claims of error in hopes that doing so in a different proceeding may change the outcome. Turrentine v. State, 1998 OK CR 44, { 12, 965 P.2d 985, 989. “Simply envisioning a new method of presenting an argument previously raised does not avoid the procedural bar.” McCarty v. State, 1999 OK CR 24, 9 9, 989 P.2d 990,995. “Appellate jurisprudence was not created or designed to allow a person convicted of a crime to continually challenge a conviction with new assertions of error.” Mayes v. State, 1996 OK CR 28, § 14, n.3, 921 P.2d 367, 372, n.3.

Many of the claims raised in Petitioner’s application for post-conviction relief involve issues that either were or could have been
raised in his direct appeal and are thus barred by res judicata or waived. 22 O.S.Supp.2022, § 1086; King, 2001 OK CR 29, § 4, 29 P.3d at 1090. He has not established any sufficient reason for not asserting or inadequately raising these grounds for relief in his direct appeal. Id.

Petitioner’s claim of ineffective assistance of appellate counsel is appropriate for review in a post-conviction application because this is the first opportunity for such review. See Logan v. State, 2013 OK CR 2, 4 5, 293 P.2d 969, 973. As set forth in Logan, post-conviction claims of ineffective assistance of appellate counsel are reviewed under the two-prong standard set forth in Strickland v. Washington, 466 U.S. 668 (1984). Id. To prevail on his ineffectiveness assistance claim, Petitioner must show that appellant counsel’s performance was deficient and that it prejudiced his defense. Strickland v. Washington, 466 U.S. 668, 687 (1984). Having reviewed Petitioner’s complaints under the Strickland analysis, we conclude that Petitioner has not rebutted the strong presumption that appellant counsel’s choices were the product of a reasonable strategy consistent with prevailing professional norms. Nor has Petitioner shown a reasonable probability that, but for these alleged errors, the result would have been different. The challenged acts and omissions do not satisfy either prong of the Strickland test.

Petitioner claims he is factually innocent. 
This Court’s rules and cases do not procedurally bar the raising of factual innocence claims in a post-conviction application. Slaughter v. State, 2005 OK CR6, 46, 108 P.3d 1052, 1054. “We fully recognize innocence claims are the Post-Conviction Procedure Act’s foundation.” Id. Claims of actual innocence may be raised for the first time on post-conviction, as it is possible that it might be a petitioner’s first opportunity to
allege and argue the issue. Braun v. State, 1997 OK CR 26, § 28 n.15, 937 P.2d 505, 514 n.15. 

The “actual innocence” exception applies only to factual innocence, where a petitioner can make a colorable showing he is actually innocent of the crime for which he is convicted. Id. 
Petitioner’s bald assertions fail to meet this standard. Petitioner does not cite any controlling authority supporting relief, and his claims are wholly unsupported by the record before this Court.

Petitioner fails to comprehend the nature of the burden a post-convictioner bears in making post-conviction claims before a trial court. 
“It is fundamental that where a post-conviction appeal is filed, the burden is upon the petitioner to sustain the allegations of his petition, and that every presumption favors the regularity of the proceedings had in the trial court. Error must affirmatively appear and is never presumed.” Russel v. Cherokee County Dist. Ct., 1968 OK CR 45, 9 5, 438 P.2d 293, 294. 

Having thoroughly reviewed Petitioner’s arguments and the record on appeal, Petitioner has not shown that the district court’s decision was clearly erroneous or clearly against the logic and effect of the facts presented.

Petitioner has failed to establish that he is entitled to post-conviction relief, the order of the District Court of Cleveland County in Case No. CF-2019-1273, denying Petitioner’s application for post-conviction relief is AFFIRMED. Pursuant to Rule 3.15, Rules of the Oklahoma Court of Criminal Appeals, Title 22, Ch.18, App. (2026), the MANDATE is ORDERED issued upon the delivery and filing of this decision.

IT IS SO ORDERED.
