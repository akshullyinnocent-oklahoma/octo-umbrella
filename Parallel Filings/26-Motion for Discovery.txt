MOTION FOR DISCOVERY OF ALL CASE-RELATED MATERIALS

COMES NOW the Petitioner, Dallas Christopher Norton, pro se, and respectfully moves this
Honorable Court for an order compelling the production of all case-related materials from the
Oklahoma Department of Human Services (DHS), Arizona Department of Child Safety (DCS),
Norman Police Department, and Attorney Keith Nedwick pursuant to 22 O.S. § 1084(b), Brady v.
Maryland, 373 U.S. 83 (1963), and the Due Process Clauses of the United States and
Oklahoma Constitutions. In support of this motion, Petitioner states:
1. Petitioner has filed an Application for Post-Conviction Relief raising substantial constitutional
claims, including actual innocence, that require access to complete case-related materials
currently in the possession of various agencies and individuals.
2. The requested materials are necessary for the full and fair adjudication of Petitioner's
post-conviction claims and were either not previously provided to the defense or were
improperly withheld.
3. Specifically, Petitioner seeks the following materials:
a. **Oklahoma Department of Human Services (DHS)**:
- Complete, unredacted DHS investigation file related to the allegations against Petitioner
- All reports, notes, and recordings of interviews with the complainant
- All communications between DHS and law enforcement regarding this case
- All communications between Oklahoma DHS and Arizona DCS
- Records of any prior or subsequent allegations involving the complainant
- Any assessments or evaluations of the complainant
- Documentation of the initial disclosure and referral process
- Names and contact information for all DHS personnel involved in the case
b. **Arizona Department of Child Safety (DCS)**:
- Complete, unredacted DCS file related to any investigations involving the complainant
- All reports, notes, and recordings of interviews with the complainant in Arizona

- Records of communications with Heather Slater, who was listed as a witness but never
called
- All communications with Oklahoma authorities regarding this case
- Any assessments or evaluations of the complainant conducted in Arizona
- Documentation of any disclosures made by the complainant to Arizona authorities
- Records of the complainant's living situation and custody arrangements in Arizona
c. **Norman Police Department**:
- Complete, unredacted investigative file, including all supplemental reports
- Original recordings of all witness interviews, including the initial disclosure
- Complete personnel file of Detective Sean Judy
- All communications between Detective Judy and the complainant or her family
- All communications between Detective Judy and prosecutors
- All communications regarding the decision not to conduct a medical examination
- Dispatch records and call logs related to this case
- Any body camera or surveillance footage related to this investigation
- Training records for Detective Judy regarding child sexual abuse investigations
- All notes, reports, and recordings related to Officer Pierce's involvement in the case
- Documentation of the chain of custody for all evidence
d. **Attorney Keith Nedwick**:
- Complete defense file, including all notes, correspondence, and work product
- Records of all witness interviews conducted or attempted
- All discovery materials received from the State
- All communications with prosecutors regarding this case
- All communications with potential witnesses
- Any investigation reports or materials gathered by the defense
- Records of subpoenas issued and responses received
- Any expert consultations or reports
- Notes from client meetings and case preparation
4. These materials are material and necessary to Petitioner's claims for the following reasons:
a. The Application alleges that the State failed to produce ordered DHS records or that Mr.
Norton was never privileged to view them. and that there was an absence of CAC-compliant
disclosure protocols.
b. The Application references missing witness Heather Slater from Arizona DCS, who was
listed on the witness list but never called to testify.
c. The Application details Detective Judy's systematic investigative failures, including his
admission that he never preserved the initial disclosure recording, never documented the crime
scene, never investigated discrepancies in the victim's statements, and never checked for
electronic evidence.

d. The Application alleges ineffective assistance of trial counsel, which requires review of the
complete defense file to assess counsel's investigation, preparation, and strategic decisions.

5. Brady v. Maryland and its progeny require the disclosure of all material exculpatory evidence,
including impeachment evidence, and this obligation continues post-conviction. See Thomas v.
Goldsmith, 979 F.2d 746, 749-50 (9th Cir. 1992).

6. Oklahoma law recognizes that post-conviction applicants are entitled to discovery when
necessary to support their claims. See 22 O.S. § 1084(b) (authorizing the court to "make such
orders as are necessary to secure the prisoner's appearance and the production of records,
documents, and witnesses necessary for the proper consideration of the application").

7. The requested materials are likely to contain exculpatory evidence supporting Petitioner's
claims of actual innocence and constitutional violations, including:
- Documentation confirming Petitioner's non-residency during the alleged timeframe
- Evidence of inconsistent statements by the complainant
- Evidence of improper investigative techniques
- Evidence of Brady violations by the prosecution

WHEREFORE, Petitioner respectfully requests that this Court:

1. Order the Oklahoma Department of Human Services, Arizona Department of Child Safety,
Norman Police Department, and Attorney Keith Nedwick to produce all case-related materials
as specified above;

2. Order that these materials be provided to Petitioner within 30 days;

3. Conduct an in camera review of any materials claimed to be privileged or confidential to
determine their relevance and potential for disclosure;

4. Appoint a special master if necessary to oversee the discovery process; and
5. Grant such other relief as the Court deems just and proper.
