MOTION TO DISQUALIFY JUDGE THAD BALKMAN
COMES NOW the Petitioner, Dallas Christopher Norton, pro se, and respectfully moves for the
disqualification of Judge Thad Balkman from presiding over any proceedings related to
Petitioner's Application for Post-Conviction Relief. In support of this motion, Petitioner states:
Judge Balkman presided over Petitioner's jury trial from June 21-24, 2022, and subsequent
sentencing on August 31, 2022, which are directly challenged in the Application for
Post-Conviction Relief.
The Application alleges specific judicial errors by Judge Balkman that violated Petitioner's
constitutional rights, including:
a. Allowing the admission of unreliable hearsay without proper review of the forensic interview
that contained material inconsistencies;
b. Permitting Detective Judy's false testimony about victim contact to stand uncorrected
despite direct contradiction by the victim's sworn VPO testimony;
c. Failing to intervene during numerous instances of prosecutorial misconduct in closing
arguments, including improper vouching, inflammatory rhetoric, burden-shifting, and
misstatements of law and fact;

d. Denying Petitioner's motion for new trial despite clear grounds under 22 O.S. § 952(4)-(7),
including the prosecutor's own admission during closing that certain hearsay evidence was "not
reliable";
e. Failing to properly instruct the jury on critical issues, including no corrective instruction after
the prosecutor improperly defined reasonable doubt.
Judge Balkman cannot impartially evaluate claims that directly challenge his own judicial
conduct and decisions.
The Oklahoma Code of Judicial Conduct, Rule 2.11(A), requires disqualification in any
proceeding where the judge's impartiality might reasonably be questioned, including when the
judge has personal knowledge of disputed evidentiary facts or has previously presided over the
matter in another court.
Due process requires not only actual impartiality but also the absence of even the appearance
of bias or conflict of interest. Caperton v. A.T. Massey Coal Co., 556 U.S. 868 (2009).
WHEREFORE, Petitioner respectfully requests that Judge Thad Balkman recuse himself from
these proceedings and that the case be reassigned to a judge who can impartially evaluate the
constitutional claims raised in the Application for Post-Conviction Relief.

MOTION TO DISQUALIFY DISTRICT ATTORNEY JENNIFER AUSTIN
COMES NOW the Petitioner, Dallas Christopher Norton, pro se, and respectfully moves for the
disqualification of District Attorney Jennifer Austin and her office from representing the State in
proceedings related to Petitioner's Application for Post-Conviction Relief. In support of this
motion, Petitioner states:
Jennifer Austin, now District Attorney, personally prosecuted Petitioner's case as an Assistant
District Attorney and is directly implicated in numerous allegations of prosecutorial misconduct
detailed in the Application for Post-Conviction Relief.
The Application alleges specific instances of misconduct by Ms. Austin that violated Petitioner's
constitutional rights, including:
a. Improper vouching for witness credibility during closing arguments, stating "We know she's
telling the truth," "We know Brionna is telling the truth," and "We know the crime occurred" in
violation of ORPC Rules 3.3(a)(1) and 3.4(e), OUJI-CR 9-20, and established case law;
b. Making inflammatory statements such as "He is an abuser, she is a victim" and "He made
the choice to be a child molester" in violation of ORPC Rules 8.4(d) and 3.4(e);

c. Misrepresenting facts by stating "Nobody ever said 2018" when Officer Pierce's police
report and Tiffany Norton's VPO testimony confirmed the timeline of November 29 – December
25, 2018;
d. Shifting the burden of proof by telling jurors "If you were unhappy with that investigation, the
remedy to that is to not say, okay, we're just going to find him not guilty... Complain to the chief,
Chief Foster. We'll give you his phone number";
e. Misstating the law by defining reasonable doubt as "like potato soup" and telling jurors what
reasonable doubt "isn't";
f. Knowingly presenting and failing to correct Detective Judy's false testimony about victim
contact;
g. Exploiting investigative failures while simultaneously arguing the investigation was
thorough.
Ms. Austin cannot ethically represent the State in evaluating claims that directly challenge her
own professional conduct and allege that she personally violated Petitioner's constitutional
rights.
The Oklahoma Rules of Professional Conduct, Rule 1.7, prohibits representation involving a
concurrent conflict of interest, including when there is a significant risk that the representation
will be materially limited by the lawyer's personal interests.
As the current District Attorney, Ms. Austin's conflict extends to her entire office, as all Assistant
District Attorneys serve under her supervision and direction.
WHEREFORE, Petitioner respectfully requests that District Attorney Jennifer Austin and the
Cleveland County District Attorney's Office be disqualified from representing the State in these
proceedings, and that the Attorney General appoint special counsel to represent the State in
this matter.
