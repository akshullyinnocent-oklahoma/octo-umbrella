COMES NOW, Petitioner, Dallas Christopher Norton, pro se, and respectfully petitions this Honorable Court for a Writ of Mandamus to compel the District Court of Cleveland County to perform its legal duties. In the alternative, Petitioner requests this Court to assume original jurisdiction and rule on the pending motions directly. In support of this Petition, Petitioner states as follows:


1. JURISDICTION AND RELIEF SOUGHT
This Petition for Writ of Mandamus is filed pursuant to this Court’s superintending control over all inferior courts in criminal cases,conferred by 22 O.S. § 1051, and the authority to issue extraordinary writs under the Oklahoma Constitution, Art. VII, § 4. Petitioner seeks an order compelling the District Court to rule on fully briefed motions or, alternatively, for this Court to rule on said motions and appoint special counsel.


2. FACTUAL AND PROCEDURAL BACKGROUND
1. On June 30, 2025, Petitioner filed his initial Application for Post-Conviction Relief.


2. On July 24, 2025, the District Court (Judge Balkman) entered an Order for Motion to Conform with District Court Rules, directing Petitioner to refile his Application to comply with local formatting rules for civil briefs.


3. While preparing the conforming application, Petitioner filed the following motions to address fundamental barriers to a fair proceeding:
   * Motion to Disqualify Judge Thad Balkman (filed 8-11-25)
   * Motion to Disqualify Judge Lori Puckett (filed 8-11-25)
   * Motion to Disqualify District Attorney Jennifer Austin (filed 8-11-25)
   * Motion for Discovery of All Case-Related Materials (filed 8-11-25)
   * Motion to Disqualify Assistant District Attorney Jacobi Whatley (filed 8-18-25)
4. Petitioner has since filed his conforming Application for Post-Conviction Relief on October 7, 2025.


5. To date, the District Court has taken no action on the five motions filed in August. It has neither scheduled hearings nor issued rulings, creating a procedural standstill.


3. ARGUMENT AND AUTHORITIES


1.  Mandamus Lies to Compel a Ruling on Pending Motions.
A Writ of Mandamus is the proper remedy to compel a lower court to perform a ministerial duty—such as ruling on motions that have been pending for an unreasonable time.Barnett v. State, 1993 OK CR 9, 853 P.2d 226. The District Court’s failure to rule on the motions filed in August 2025 is an abdication of its duty and warrants intervention by this Court.


2. The District Court is Incapable of Impartially Ruling on the Disqualification Motions, Justifying this Court's Direct Intervention.
The core of the impasse is that Petitioner has asked Judge Balkman to rule on a motion seeking his own disqualification.It is unreasonable to expect a judge to rule impartially on allegations of his own bias. See Oklahoma Code of Judicial Conduct, Rule 2.11, Cmt. [1] ("A judge is disqualified whenever the judge’s impartiality might reasonably be questioned, regardless of whether any of the specific rules in Section 2.11 apply.").


Therefore, the most just and efficient remedy is for this Court to assume original jurisdiction and rule on the merits of the Motions to Disqualify Judge Thad Balkman, Judge Lori Puckett, District Attorney Jennifer Austin, and ADA Jacobi Whatley. The motions are fully briefed and present pure questions of law regarding conflicts of interest and due process, which this Court is uniquely positioned to resolve.


3. The Conflict of Interest Requires the Appointment of Special Counsel.
Petitioner’s Post-Conviction Application alleges extensive prosecutorial misconduct by Jennifer Austin,who is now the sitting District Attorney for Cleveland County. It is a fundamental violation of due process for a prosecutor to act as an adversarial respondent when they are personally accused of misconduct that forms the basis of the appeal. See Young v. U.S. ex rel. Vuitton et Fils S.A., 481 U.S. 787, 814 (1987).


The Oklahoma Rules of Professional Conduct, Rule 1.7, prohibits representation involving a concurrent conflict of interest, which is present here. Ms. Austin and her entire office cannot ethically evaluate claims against their own integrity.


WHEREFORE, Petitioner respectfully prays that this Honorable Court:


1. Assume original jurisdiction over this matter.


2. Issue a Writ of Mandamus directing the District Court of Cleveland County to immediately issue rulings on Petitioner’s Motion for Discovery of All Case-Related Materials and to show cause for its delay.


3. In the alternative, and as the preferred remedy, this Court should directly rule on the merits of Petitioner’s:
   *    Motion to Disqualify Judge Thad Balkman;
   *    Motion to Disqualify Judge Lori Puckett;
   *    Motion to Disqualify District Attorney Jennifer Austin; and
   *    Motion to Disqualify Assistant District Attorney Jacobi Whatley.


4. Order the appointment of a special counsel from the Oklahoma Attorney General’s office, or another impartial entity, to represent the State of Oklahoma in all further proceedings related to Petitioner’s Application for Post-Conviction Relief.


5. Grant such other and further relief as this Court deems just and proper.
