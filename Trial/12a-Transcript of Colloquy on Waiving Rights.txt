**DALLAS NORTON**
was called as a witness and, after having been first duly sworn, testified as follows:
#### **DIRECT EXAMINATION**
**BY MR. DOUGLAS:**
**Q.** Mr. Nedwick has represented you for a number of years now, hasn't he?
**A.** Yes, sir.
**Q.** In this case alone. Is that correct?
**A.** Yes.
**Q.** And I've been part of it for quite some time as well. Is that correct?
**A.** Yes.
**Q.** And have you had Mr. Nedwick's advice regarding the calling of witnesses and the preparation of a defense?
**A.** Yes.

**Q.** And we've discussed any number of possible witnesses, haven't we?
**A.** Yes.
**Q.** And up until this date, we've spent a lot of time preparing, but three or four actually made the cut before we showed up for trial this week. Is that correct?
**A.** Yes.
**Q.** And we've — Mr. Nedwick has issued the subpoenas and had everybody prepared, and they're here and ready to go. Do you understand that?
**A.** Yes.
**Q.** And have you felt any pressure from Mr. Nedwick or myself in respect to either preparing the defense or calling witnesses?
**A.** No.
**Q.** I understand that you — I understand that you choose not to call any of the witnesses that we've talked about, notwithstanding we've discussed them for years, that you do choose not to call any witnesses on your own behalf at this time. Is that correct?
**A.** Yes.
**Q.** And has Mr. Nedwick or myself at all ever pressured you into making the decision not to call witnesses?
**A.** No.
**Q.** And this can be based on your interpretation that the State's failed to prove their case. Is that correct?
**A.** Yes.

**A.** Yes.

**A.** Yes.

**Q.** And do you feel like you've been properly advised on the rights that you have with respect to your testimony?
**A.** Yes.
